Storm Is Not a Workflow Engine/ESB/Hadoop Replacement

Apr 30th, 2013

I have come across a lot people at work, conferences and on the Storm mailing list who tend toward comparing Storm to workflow engines and ESBs.

In both cases such a comparison is invalid, and comparable to comparing a wrench to a hammer. Yes, you can probably drive a nail with a wrench in a pinch, but the results will be far from optimal or efficient. You’re much better off using the right tool for the job.

At first glance, Storm has several similarities with Workflow Engines, ESBs, and Map-Reduce frameworks: It has primitives that allow you to define conditional flows, filtering, and map-reduce-type operations. But those similarities are much like those that apply to the hammer/wrench analogy.

Instead of thinking of storm as a replacement for these technologies, think of storm as a complementary technology.

Storm began (and is still used) as a framework to perform analytics computations on twitter data streams.

Think about that for a minute. Millions of twitter users are continually posting data (there’s a reason they call it the firehose). There is no beginning. There is no end. It is a constant stream of data.
